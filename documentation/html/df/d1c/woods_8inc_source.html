13  Notes on layered textures T_Wood1 through T_Wood30:
14 
15  The pigment definitions:
16  ========================
17  Declares pigments P_Woodgrain1A through P_Woodgrain19A and
18  P_Woodgrain1B through P_Woodgrain19B. The only difference between the
19  "A" designations and the "B" designations is in design. The "A"'s
20  were designed to generally work better as underlying patterns, using
21  opaque color_maps, while the "B" pigments were designed to work
22  better as top layers, with some degree of transparency in their
23  color_maps.
24 
25  The pigments with the "A" and "B" designations are combinations that
26  were designed to work well together, but do not neccessarily have to
27  be used as pairs.
28 
29  These pigment definitions do not have colormaps assigned so
30  that they can mix 'n matched in various combinations by the user when
31  designing their own colors. In this file, the color_maps are added at
32  the point of defining the final textures.
33 
34 
35  The color_map definitions:
36  ==========================
37  These are declared in the #include file "woodmaps.inc". They are
38  named M_Wood1A through M_Wood19A and M_Wood1B through M_Wood19B.
39 
40  There are two types of declarations in woodmaps.inc. The "A" color_maps
41  are intended as the 1st layer of multi-layer textures, but many work
42  well by themselves, as well.
43 
44  The "B" color_maps contain transparent colors and are intended for
45  use as top layers to influence the "A" maps.
46 
47 
48  The wood textures
49  =================
50  Here the various P_WoodGrainx textures are combined with color_maps to
51  create textures named T_Wood1 through T_Wood30.
52 
53 
54  General
55  =======
56  Wood textures are designed with the major axis of the woodgrain
57  "cylinder" aligned along the Z axis. With the exception of the few
58  of the textures below that have a small amount of x-axis rotation
59  built-in, the textures below will exhibit a very straight grain
60  pattern unless you apply a small amount of x-axis rotation to them
61  (generally 2 to 4 degrees seems to work well).
62 */
